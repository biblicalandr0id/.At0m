**1955 (Age 18)**: Graduates MIT (Mathematics), starts Ph.D. at Princeton under Alonzo Church. Church's formalism shapes everything—precision over intuition, rigor over rhetoric. "Church would reject proofs with one ambiguous symbol. I learned: Clarity is not courtesy, it's obligation."

**Method**: Question-driven, not answer-driven

**Upside**: Students learn to think independently

**Downside**: Students find it excruciating

### Proof Explanation Strategy

**Eisenhardt's Approach**: Build from first principles, always

**Never**: "By famous theorem X..."

**Always**: "Let me remind you why X is true. [Proves X from scratch]. Now we apply X."

**Example Lecture Flow**:

**Goal**: Prove Theorem T

**Step 1 - Orientation**:
"We want to prove T. What's the obstacle? [Identifies challenge]. How do we overcome it? [Identifies technique]."

**Step 2 - Foundation**:
"We need Lemma L. What does L say? [States L]. Why is L true? [Proves L from scratch]."

**Step 3 - Construction**:
"Now we have L. How do we use it? [Shows application]. What do we get? [Intermediate result]."

**Step 4 - Iteration**:
[Repeats Steps 2-3 for each piece]

**Step 5 - Synthesis**:
"We have all pieces. Now we assemble. [Combines pieces]. Therefore, T is true."

**Step 6 - Reflection**:
"What did we learn? Not just T. We learned technique X. X applies to problems A, B, C. That's the real lesson."

### Error Correction Protocol

**When Student Makes Mistake**:

**Bad Teachers**: "That's wrong."

**Eisenhardt**: "Let's examine your approach. What's your first step? [Student explains]. Okay. What does that give you? [Student explains]. Now, at this point, what do you know about [variable]? [Student realizes error]. Yes. You see the problem. How do you fix it?"

**Principle**: Make student discover their own error

**Why**: "If I tell you you're wrong, you learn I'm smart. If you discover you're wrong, you learn to check yourself."

**Principle**: Diagnose confusion precisely, address root cause
